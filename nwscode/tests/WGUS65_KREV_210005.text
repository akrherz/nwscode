THE FLASH FLOOD WATCH CONTINUES FOR

* A PORTION OF WESTERN NEVADA...INCLUDING THE FOLLOWING AREA...
  IN WESTERN NEVADA...GREATER RENO-CARSON CITY-MINDEN AREA.

* UNTIL 9 PM PDT THIS EVENING

* SCATTERED THUNDERSTORMS WERE PRODUCING LOCALLY HEAVY RAINFALL
  OVER WESTERN NEVADA THIS AFTERNOON...MAINLY ALONG HIGHWAY 50
  FROM CARSON CITY TO NEAR STAGECOACH. RAINFALL RATES AROUND AN
  INCH PER HOUR HAVE OCCURRED NEAR DAYTON THIS AFTERNOON. HEAVY
  RAINFALL OF AROUND 2 INCHES PER HOUR REMAIN A POSSIBILITY WITH
  STORMS THROUGH EARLY EVENING.

* A FLASH FLOOD WATCH MEANS THAT FLOODING OF SMALL STREAMS... 
  CREEKS AND OTHER DRAINAGE AREAS IS POSSIBLE WITHIN THE WATCH 
  AREA. RECENTLY BURNED AREAS SUCH AS THE JACKASS AND LINEHAN 
  BURNS ARE ESPECIALLY SUSCEPTIBLE TO FLASH FLOODING. PEOPLE IN 
  THE WATCH AREA SHOULD BE AWARE OF HEAVY RAINFALL AND BE 
  PREPARED FOR IMMEDIATE ACTION SHOULD HEAVY RAINS AND FLOODING 
  OCCUR OR A FLASH FLOOD WARNING BE ISSUED. 

NEVER DRIVE YOUR VEHICLE INTO AREAS WHERE THE WATER COVERS THE
ROADWAY. THE WATER DEPTH MAY BE TOO GREAT TO ALLOW YOUR CAR TO
CROSS SAFELY.
